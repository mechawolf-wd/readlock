# Course Structure Ideas

Ideas to break packages out of the same intro-stories-question-reveal-truefalse-reflect-outro shape. Not implemented yet.

---

1. **True/False hook**, Open with a quick @true_false instead of @text intro. Obvious, general, short consequences. Then straight into Phase 2.

2. **Quote opener**, @quote replaces the intro. One grounded line from a real person. Phase 2 starts right after.

3. **Estimate opener**, @estimate before any context. The reader guesses a number blind, then the stories explain it.

4. **Cold-open story**, No intro at all. First swipe is Phase 2 mid-scene. Works when the story is strong enough to carry the opening.

5. **Reversed reveal**, Name the concept first, then show stories that prove it. "Claim then evidence" instead of "curiosity then understanding."

6. **Single long story**, One continuous narrative from Phase 2 through Phase 4. The concept name appears inside the story, not in a separate reveal.

7. **Misconception-first**, Open with a wrong belief stated as fact. Let the reader sit with it. Phase 2 breaks it. Phase 4 explains why it felt true.

8. **Dialogue structure**, Phase 2 as a back-and-forth between two positions. Each swipe is one side. Phase 3 asks which side the reader agrees with.

9. **Data-first**, Open with a raw number in a @pause or @text. No context. Phase 2 stories explain what it means.

10. **Outro-as-bridge**, The outro of one package is the opening thought of the next. No seam between packages.

11. **Question-first**, Open with a @question before any stories. The reader commits to an answer with no information, then Phase 2 shows why their guess was probably wrong.

12. **Image-led**, Open with an image-link in the first @text swipe. One line of text, then the image. The visual sets the tone instead of words.

13. **Two reveals**, Split Phase 4 across two @text swipes. First swipe names the concept. Second swipe shows why the obvious interpretation of it is incomplete.

14. **No question package**, Skip Phase 3 entirely. Phase 2 stories flow straight into Phase 4 reveal. Works when the pattern is so clear that a question would feel patronizing.

15. **Reflect-first closer**, Move @reflect before Phase 5 instead of after. The reader connects the concept to their life before the true/false tests it. Changes the order from "test then think" to "think then test."
