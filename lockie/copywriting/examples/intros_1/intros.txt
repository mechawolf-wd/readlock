INTRO 1
Topic: memory
Opener style: historical sweep
Ending: literal question

For most of human history, nobody wrote anything down. Songs, family lines,
and recipes lived inside people's heads, passed from one mouth to the next.
A grandfather could list every ancestor going back ten generations without
stopping. So why does the same brain now struggle to remember a phone number?

INTRO 2
Topic: morning habits
Opener style: universal observation
Ending: literal question

People will swear they want to change something, then do the same thing
tomorrow morning. The alarm rings, and the hand reaches for the phone before
the eyes are open. By the time the coffee is poured, last night's plan is
already gone. Why does the same person who meant it lose every morning before
breakfast?

INTRO 3
Topic: seeing faces in objects
Opener style: gentle question
Ending: literal question

You have probably noticed a face in places where there is no face. It might
be a car's grille, a wall socket, or the foam on a coffee cup. Your eyes
lock onto it before you decide to look. What is the brain so afraid of
missing?

INTRO 4
Topic: colour and language
Opener style: grounded fact
Ending: literal question

The human eye sees roughly ten million colours, but only a few hundred have
names. The rest live inside the head without ever being labelled. We have
all stood in a paint shop holding two strips that look identical, certain
one was wrong. So what is the eye seeing that the words cannot catch?

INTRO 5
Topic: small daily spending
Opener style: universal observation
Ending: unfinished "but" (not a literal question)

Most people can tell you exactly what they spent on a coat last winter. They
have no idea what they spent on lunch this week. The big number sticks; the
hundred small ones disappear the moment the receipt hits the bin. But added
up, the small ones almost always cost more than the coat.
